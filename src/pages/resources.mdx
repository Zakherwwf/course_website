# Supporting Materials

This section provides a central hub for all supporting materials for the course, including a glossary, reading lists, online resources, and field guides.

## Glossary of Key Terms

<div class="glossary">
<dl>
    <dt>Adaptation</dt><dd>Adjustment in natural or human systems in response to actual or expected climatic stimuli or their effects.</dd>
    <dt>Agrobiodiversity</dt><dd>The variety and variability of plants, animals and micro-organisms used directly or indirectly for food and agriculture.</dd>
    <dt>Biodiversity Hotspot</dt><dd>Biogeographic region with significant levels of biodiversity threatened by human habitation.</dd>
    <dt>Blue Carbon</dt><dd>Carbon captured by ocean and coastal ecosystems, primarily mangroves, seagrasses, and salt marshes.</dd>
    <dt>Ecosystem Services</dt><dd>Benefits people obtain from ecosystems (provisioning, regulating, cultural, supporting).</dd>
    <dt>Endemic Species</dt><dd>Species native to and found only within limited geographic area.</dd>
    <dt>Gdel</dt><dd>Traditional North African grazing management system involving seasonal pasture resting.</dd>
    <dt>Keystone Species</dt><dd>Species with a disproportionately large effect on its environment relative to its abundance.</dd>
    <dt>Nature-Based Solutions</dt><dd>Actions protecting, managing and restoring ecosystems to address societal challenges.</dd>
    <dt>Payment for Ecosystem Services (PES)</dt><dd>Economic incentives offered to landowners for maintaining ecosystem services.</dd>
    <dt>Sclerophyllous</dt><dd>Having hard, thick, waxy evergreen leaves adapted to hot, dry conditions.</dd>
    <dt>Traditional Ecological Knowledge (TEK)</dt><dd>Knowledge, practices and beliefs of indigenous and local communities about relationships with their environment.</dd>
</dl>
</div>
